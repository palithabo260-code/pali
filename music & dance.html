Description of traditional dances and instruments

Mohobelo dance

The Mohobelo is a vigorous stomping dance performed mainly by Basotho men. Dancers form a line and stamp the ground in unison, creating a powerful rhythm that expresses strength and unity.

Ndlamo dance

Ndlamo is a ceremonial dance performed during important events and rituals. It is often accompanied by chants and drumbeats, emphasizing both storytelling and celebration.

Lekatola dram

The Lekatola is a traditional drum used in Basotho performances. It produces deep, resonant sounds that guide the dancers’ movements and maintain rhythm during ceremonies.

Sechaba horns

The Sechaba horns were historically blown to signal gatherings, celebrations, or important announcements.

Kalimba

The Kalimba, though borrowed from neighboring groups, has been adopted in Basotho music for its melodic tones.

Koriana

The Accordion, introduced later, became central in modern Basotho music, blending with traditional songs and dances.

Moropa/ dram

The Moropa /Drum is widely used in dances to set rhythm, particularly in initiation and celebration ceremonies.

Setolo-tolo

The Setolo-tolo is a jaw harp used to create rhythmic, twanging sounds for entertainment.

Lesiba

The Lesiba is a mouth-resonated instrument made from a quill and string, producing buzzing tones.

Thomo

The Thomo is a musical bow played mainly by women, producing soft and spiritual sounds

Lekolulo

The Lekolulo is a flute made from sorghum stalks, often played by herdboys while watching livestock.

Mokhibo

The Mokhibo is a graceful women’s dance performed while kneeling, where dancers use hand and upper body movements to express beauty and storytelling.

Mokorotlo dance

Mokorotlo is both a war song and a dance, performed by men with shields and sticks to celebrate bravery and leadership.

Setapo dance

The Setapo dance involves women forming a line and moving rhythmically, clapping and singing together.

Thsomo

They are stories told by the elderly people in order to advice young children.

Sekoch dance

Initiation dance are performed by boys and girls who have completed traditional rites of passage.

Makhele horn

Makhele horn is made from animal horn and produces a sharp, resonant note for ceremonies.

Mokhibo drum

Mokhibo drum is hand-held and played during family ceremonies, marking rhythm and pace.

Moropa-tsoho

Moropa-tsoho is a small drum used by children learning traditional music and dance.

Liphiri dance

Liphiring dance is a celebratory group dance performed after successful hunts or cattle drives.

Morena dance

Morena dance is performed during royal ceremonies, emphasizing respect and honor through slow, deliberate movements.

Mothoana dance

Mothoana dance is a courtship dance, emphasizing grace, poise, and eye contact between dancers.
