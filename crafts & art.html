Mohope

Mohope is the Sesotho word for a calabash, a traditional container made from the dried fruit of the calabash tree. It has been used for centuries in Basotho culture.
Purpose: Mohope is used mainly for drinking traditional beer (joala ba Sesotho), milk, or water. Because it is natural and hollow inside, it works like a cup or bow. further reading

.

Seroto

Seroto in Basotho culture is a traditional wooden mortar used together with a pestle (lehloko) for pounding or grinding food.
Purpose: Seroto is mainly used for pounding maize, sorghum, and other grains into flour, or for softening food like peanuts and herbs.

Sefaha

Sefaha is a traditional Basotho necklace, usually made from beads, animal bones, shells, or sometimes seeds.
Design: Sefaha can be colorful and patterned, showing the creativity of Basotho beadwork. further reading

.

Mokotla

Mokotla is a traditional Basotho bag usually woven from strong grass, leather, or animal skin.
Purpose: It is used to carry personal items such as food, herbs, or small tools. In the past, shepherds (balisa) carried a mokotla when looking after animals in the mountains. further reading

.

Molamu

Molamu is a traditional Basotho stick or staff, usually carved from strong wood.
Cultural role: Beyond being practical, the molamu has symbolic meaning. It represents strength, authority, and manhood in Basotho culture. It is also used in traditional stick fighting (mokholo or ndebele style) as a form of sport, self-defense, and cultural expression. further reading
